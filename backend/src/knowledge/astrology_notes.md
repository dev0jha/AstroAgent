# Astrology Reference — Aradhana Knowledge Base

## 1. The 12 Zodiac Signs

**Aries (March 21 – April 19)**
Element: Fire | Modality: Cardinal | Ruling Planet: Mars
Keywords: initiative, courage, pioneering, impulsive, bold.
Strengths: Leadership, enthusiasm, confidence, and willingness to take risks. Aries natives often blaze new trails and inspire others with their fearless energy.
Shadow: Impatience, aggression, selfishness, and a tendency to start projects without finishing them.

**Taurus (April 20 – May 20)**
Element: Earth | Modality: Fixed | Ruling Planet: Venus
Keywords: stability, sensuality, persistence, reliability, beauty.
Strengths: Endurance, loyalty, practical wisdom, appreciation for beauty and comfort. Taurus builds slowly but builds to last.
Shadow: Stubbornness, possessiveness, resistance to change, overindulgence in material pleasures.

**Gemini (May 21 – June 20)**
Element: Air | Modality: Mutable | Ruling Planet: Mercury
Keywords: communication, curiosity, duality, adaptability, wit.
Strengths: Quick thinking, versatility, social intelligence, and the gift of storytelling. Gemini thrives on information exchange.
Shadow: Inconsistency, superficiality, anxiety, indecision, and a tendency to scatter energy.

**Cancer (June 21 – July 22)**
Element: Water | Modality: Cardinal | Ruling Planet: Moon
Keywords: nurturing, emotion, home, intuition, memory.
Strengths: Deep empathy, loyalty, emotional intelligence, protective instincts. Cancer creates safe spaces for those they love.
Shadow: Moodiness, clinginess, hypersensitivity, and withdrawal when hurt.

**Leo (July 23 – August 22)**
Element: Fire | Modality: Fixed | Ruling Planet: Sun
Keywords: creativity, leadership, generosity, pride, performance.
Strengths: Charisma, warmth, creative expression, and natural leadership. Leo shines brightest when helping others see their own light.
Shadow: Ego inflation, need for validation, dominance, and jealousy.

**Virgo (August 23 – September 22)**
Element: Earth | Modality: Mutable | Ruling Planet: Mercury
Keywords: analysis, service, discernment, health, precision.
Strengths: Attention to detail, practical helpfulness, analytical brilliance, and devotion to craft and wellbeing.
Shadow: Over-criticism (of self and others), anxiety, perfectionism that paralyzes.

**Libra (September 23 – October 22)**
Element: Air | Modality: Cardinal | Ruling Planet: Venus
Keywords: balance, relationships, justice, diplomacy, beauty.
Strengths: Fairness, aesthetic sense, grace in relationships, ability to see all sides. Libra seeks harmony above all.
Shadow: Indecisiveness, people-pleasing, avoidance of conflict at the cost of truth.

**Scorpio (October 23 – November 21)**
Element: Water | Modality: Fixed | Ruling Planets: Mars (traditional), Pluto (modern)
Keywords: transformation, depth, power, mystery, regeneration.
Strengths: Psychological insight, intensity, resilience, and the capacity for profound transformation and healing.
Shadow: Jealousy, control, obsession, and the tendency to hold grudges across lifetimes.

**Sagittarius (November 22 – December 21)**
Element: Fire | Modality: Mutable | Ruling Planet: Jupiter
Keywords: philosophy, freedom, adventure, optimism, truth-seeking.
Strengths: Broad vision, love of wisdom, infectious optimism, and a spirit that refuses to be caged.
Shadow: Tactlessness, over-promise, restlessness, and dogmatism disguised as open-mindedness.

**Capricorn (December 22 – January 19)**
Element: Earth | Modality: Cardinal | Ruling Planet: Saturn
Keywords: ambition, discipline, responsibility, mastery, tradition.
Strengths: Determination, patience, strategic thinking, and an unwavering commitment to long-term goals.
Shadow: Coldness, workaholism, fear of vulnerability, and rigidity.

**Aquarius (January 20 – February 18)**
Element: Air | Modality: Fixed | Ruling Planets: Saturn (traditional), Uranus (modern)
Keywords: innovation, community, rebellion, humanitarianism, originality.
Strengths: Visionary thinking, commitment to collective progress, intellectual independence.
Shadow: Emotional detachment, rebelliousness for its own sake, superiority complex.

**Pisces (February 19 – March 20)**
Element: Water | Modality: Mutable | Ruling Planets: Jupiter (traditional), Neptune (modern)
Keywords: compassion, imagination, spirituality, dissolution, empathy.
Strengths: Boundless empathy, artistic gift, spiritual sensitivity, and the ability to transcend ego.
Shadow: Escapism, martyrdom, difficulty with boundaries, and susceptibility to illusion.

## 2. The 10 Planets

**Sun** — Core identity, vitality, ego, life purpose. In the natal chart it shows who you are becoming. In transit it illuminates the house it occupies for roughly one month, activating themes of self-expression there.

**Moon** — Emotions, instincts, nurturing needs, the subconscious, and early conditioning. Natally it reveals emotional patterns. Transiting Moon moves fast (~2.5 days per sign), creating daily emotional weather.

**Mercury** — Thought, communication, learning, siblings, local travel. Natal Mercury shows how you think and speak. Transit Mercury activates the mind; its retrograde periods (3x/year) call for review rather than new launches.

**Venus** — Love, values, beauty, money, relationships. Natal Venus shows what and who you attract. Transit Venus softens whatever house it moves through, bringing grace, art, and connection.

**Mars** — Drive, desire, courage, conflict, sexuality, action. Natal Mars shows how you pursue what you want. Transit Mars energizes but can also inflame; in hard aspect it can trigger conflict or frustration.

**Jupiter** — Expansion, abundance, philosophy, luck, growth. Jupiter transits (~1 year per sign) bring opportunities, optimism, and growth in the house it occupies — but also excess if unchecked.

**Saturn** — Discipline, limitation, structure, karma, time, mastery. Saturn transits (~2.5 years per sign) bring tests, responsibility, and ultimately wisdom earned through effort.

**Uranus** — Disruption, innovation, liberation, awakening, rebellion. Uranus transits (~7 years per sign) correlate with sudden change, genius breakthroughs, and breaking free from stagnation.

**Neptune** — Spirituality, illusion, dissolution, creativity, compassion, fog. Neptune transits (~14 years per sign) dissolve boundaries, inspire art and mysticism, but can also bring confusion or escapism.

**Pluto** — Transformation, power, death/rebirth, shadow work, regeneration. Pluto transits (~20 years per sign) correlate with profound, irreversible change at collective and personal levels.

## 3. The 12 Houses

**House 1 (Ascendant)** — Self, physical appearance, first impressions, how the world perceives you. Natural ruler: Aries / Mars. Key question: Who am I becoming?

**House 2** — Personal resources, money, material values, self-worth, possessions. Natural ruler: Taurus / Venus. Key question: What do I value and own?

**House 3** — Communication, siblings, short journeys, local environment, early education, writing. Natural ruler: Gemini / Mercury. Key question: How do I think and connect locally?

**House 4 (IC)** — Home, roots, family of origin, ancestry, the private self, real estate. Natural ruler: Cancer / Moon. Key question: Where do I belong?

**House 5** — Creativity, romance, children, play, self-expression, pleasure, speculation. Natural ruler: Leo / Sun. Key question: How do I create and enjoy?

**House 6** — Daily routines, health, service, work environment, habits, pets. Natural ruler: Virgo / Mercury. Key question: How do I refine myself through daily practice?

**House 7 (Descendant)** — Partnerships (romantic, business), open enemies, the "other," contracts. Natural ruler: Libra / Venus. Key question: Who do I partner with and what do I project onto others?

**House 8** — Shared resources, sexuality, death, transformation, inheritance, the occult, other people's money. Natural ruler: Scorpio / Pluto. Key question: What must I release to be reborn?

**House 9** — Higher education, philosophy, long-distance travel, religion, publishing, law, foreign cultures. Natural ruler: Sagittarius / Jupiter. Key question: What beliefs expand my horizon?

**House 10 (Midheaven/MC)** — Career, public reputation, life calling, authority figures. Natural ruler: Capricorn / Saturn. Key question: What is my contribution to the world?

**House 11** — Friendships, groups, communities, hopes, ideals, social causes, networks. Natural ruler: Aquarius / Uranus. Key question: What do I dream for humanity?

**House 12** — The unconscious, hidden matters, self-undoing, solitude, spiritual retreats, karma, past lives. Natural ruler: Pisces / Neptune. Key question: What must I surrender to evolve?

## 4. Major Aspects

**Conjunction (0°, orb ±8°)** — Planets blend and amplify each other. Can be powerfully focused or overwhelming depending on the planets involved. Keywords: fusion, intensity, focus.

**Sextile (60°, orb ±4°)** — Harmonious opportunity aspect. Planets work together easily but require some initiative to activate. Keywords: opportunity, ease, talent.

**Square (90°, orb ±6°)** — Friction, challenge, and growth through tension. Forces action and change. Keywords: tension, crisis, motivation, breakthrough.

**Trine (120°, orb ±6°)** — Natural flow and harmony. Gifts may be taken for granted. Keywords: flow, ease, grace, natural ability.

**Opposition (180°, orb ±8°)** — Polarity, awareness through relationship with the "other." Can create balance or projection. Keywords: awareness, projection, balance, tension.

## 5. Retrograde Planets

A planet is retrograde when it appears to move backward in the sky from Earth's perspective — this is an optical illusion caused by orbital mechanics.

**In the natal chart:** A retrograde planet at birth suggests its energy is more internalized, reflective, and sometimes delayed in expression. The native may revisit or rework themes of that planet throughout life, finding mastery through inner experience rather than outward performance.

**In transit:** A transiting retrograde is a collective invitation to review, revise, and reflect on matters ruled by that planet.

**Inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Mars):** Their retrogrades are shorter (3 weeks to 2.5 months) and more immediately felt in daily life — communication snags, relationship reviews, slowed action.

**Outer planets (Jupiter through Pluto):** Retrograde for several months each year. Their retrograde periods shift collective energy inward — introspection on expansion, restructuring systems, reviewing power dynamics, etc. Individual effects are felt most strongly when the retrograde planet closely aspects a natal planet.

## 6. Transit Interpretation Principles

**Orb:** The degree of separation between a transiting planet and a natal point. Tighter orbs (under 1°) = stronger effect. Outer planets exact-to-natal create the most significant life events.

**Applying vs Separating:** An applying transit is approaching exactness — the energy is building. A separating transit has passed exact — the peak has occurred and the energy is waning.

**Outer planet transits (Jupiter–Pluto):** These are the major life-chapter makers. A Jupiter transit brings expansion and opportunity over months. Saturn transits test and mature. Uranus brings sudden change or liberation. Neptune dissolves structures for spiritual growth. Pluto transforms at the root level — nothing is the same afterward.

**Inner planet transits (Sun–Mars):** These act as triggers or timing agents. A transiting Mars square natal Venus might bring a few days of interpersonal tension. A transiting Sun conjunct natal Jupiter might bring a day of opportunity and visibility.

**Reading a transit:** Always consider the natal house being activated, the planet transiting, the aspect being formed, and the natal planet being aspected. The combination of all four gives the full picture of what themes, challenges, or gifts are arriving in this season of life.
